\begin{abstract}
Learning compact, physics-grounded visual representations is a central challenge for building robotic world models that jointly predict future observations and actions.
Existing approaches either reconstruct raw pixels---wasting capacity on task-irrelevant details---or use frozen pretrained features that discard action-relevant geometric information.
We introduce \textbf{Action-Conditioned Unified Latents (AC-UL)}, a framework that extends the Unified Latents (UL) variational objective to autoregressive state-action dynamics.
Given a history of observations $\vo_{t-H+1:t}$, past actions $\va_{t-H:t-1}$, and a language instruction $\bm{l}$, our dynamics prior \emph{jointly} generates the current action latent $\vb_t$ and the next observation latent $\vz_{t+1}$---the frame that results from executing the action---unifying world modeling and policy learning in a single generative process.
Both observations and actions are encoded into compact latent spaces via learned encoders and denoised with a shared diffusion objective.
By conditioning the diffusion prior on the observation-action latent history and language goal, the variational bound \emph{mathematically forces} the encoders to discard unpredictable content while retaining the physical geometry that the history of interactions can predict.
In Stage~1, the observation encoder, action encoder, dynamics prior, and diffusion decoder are jointly trained; in Stage~2, the frozen encoders provide a physics-grounded vocabulary on which an autoregressive diffusion transformer is trained for joint video-action generation, enabling closed-loop planning via iterative rollouts.
We evaluate AC-UL on simulated manipulation benchmarks (CALVIN, LIBERO) and real-world robotic video (DROID), demonstrating improvements over pixel-reconstruction world models and frozen-feature approaches in both planning accuracy and policy performance.
\end{abstract}

%=====================================================================
\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%=====================================================================

Building world models---internal simulators that predict how actions change the environment---is a long-standing goal in robotics and reinforcement learning~\citep{ha2018world,hafner2023dreamerv3}.
A world model that operates in a learned latent space can be orders of magnitude cheaper to roll out than a pixel-level simulator, enabling model-predictive control, planning, and data-efficient policy learning.

Recent progress in autoregressive video-action models~\citep{dreamzero2025} has shown that jointly generating future video frames and robot actions from a shared backbone can achieve remarkable generalization.
Meanwhile, latent diffusion models~\citep{rombach2022high} and vision-language-action (VLA) architectures~\citep{brohan2023rt2,kim2024openvla} have produced powerful visual representations.
However, two fundamental problems remain:

\textbf{(1) Pixel-reconstruction world models} (e.g., DreamerV3~\citep{hafner2023dreamerv3}, DreamZero~\citep{dreamzero2025}) spend capacity encoding textures, lighting, and background clutter that are irrelevant to the dynamics.
The learned latent confounds physical geometry with appearance, making long-horizon rollouts noisy and planning unreliable.

\textbf{(2) Frozen-feature approaches} (e.g., DINO-WM~\citep{zhou2024dinowm}) use pretrained encoders (DINOv2) that were not trained to understand action effects.
While these features transfer surprisingly well, they are agnostic to the causal structure of manipulation.

We propose a third path: \textbf{learning the latent representation itself to encode only the information that the history of interactions can predict}.
Our approach extends the Unified Latents (UL) framework~\citep{heek2025ul} from unconditional image modeling to \emph{joint} observation-action dynamics.
The key insight is that by encoding both observations and actions into learned latent spaces and conditioning the diffusion prior on the latent history and a language instruction, any encoder information that cannot be predicted from this context will spike the variational loss.
Both the action latent $\vb_t$ and the observation latent $\vz_{t+1}$ are denoised with the same diffusion objective, ensuring a unified generative process.
The encoders are thus incentivized to discard unpredictable content and retain only the physical geometry that the robot's interaction history and task goal can explain.

This formulation---where the model encodes both observations and actions into latent spaces, takes in the latent history $(\vz_{t-H+1:t}, \vb_{t-H:t-1}, \bm{l})$, and jointly denoises $(\vb_t, \vz_{t+1})$---follows the autoregressive structure of DreamZero~\citep{dreamzero2025}, but operates in a principled latent space for \emph{both} modalities rather than raw pixels and actions.

Our contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item We derive the \textbf{Action-Conditioned Unified Latent (AC-UL)} objective, extending the UL variational bound to history-conditioned \emph{joint} observation-action dynamics with learned action encoding and a unified diffusion objective for both modalities (\Cref{sec:method}).
  \item We provide a complete \textbf{two-stage training algorithm}: Stage~1 jointly trains the observation encoder, action encoder, history-conditioned dynamics prior, and diffusion decoder; Stage~2 trains an autoregressive diffusion transformer on frozen latents for joint video-action generation (\Cref{sec:stage2}).
  \item We propose and evaluate three downstream applications: \textbf{closed-loop planning via latent rollouts}, \textbf{imitation learning with joint world-action modeling} (compared to FLARE~\citep{nvidia2025flare}), and \textbf{real-world video prediction} on the DROID dataset (\Cref{sec:experiments}).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Background}
\label{sec:background}
%=====================================================================

\subsection{Variational Autoencoders and the ELBO}

Given data $\vx$ and latent variable $\vz_0$, the Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO) on the log-likelihood is:
\begin{equation}
  -\log p_\theta(\vx) \leq \E_{\vz_0 \sim p_\theta(\vz_0|\vx)}\!\Big[\underbrace{-\log p_\theta(\vx|\vz_0)}_{\text{decoder}}\Big] + \KL\!\Big[\underbrace{p_\theta(\vz_0|\vx)}_{\text{encoder}} \,\Big\|\, \underbrace{p_\theta(\vz_0)}_{\text{prior}}\Big] = \loss(\vx).
  \label{eq:elbo}
\end{equation}
In the Unified Latents framework, both the decoder $p_\theta(\vx|\vz_0)$ and the prior $p_\theta(\vz_0)$ are parameterized as diffusion models.

\subsection{Diffusion Models and the KL Bound}

Consider a destruction process $\vx_t = \alpha(t)\,\vx + \sigma(t)\,\vep$ with $\vep \sim \mathcal{N}(\bm{0},\bm{I})$, where the noise level is characterized by the log signal-to-noise ratio $\lambda(t) = \log(\alpha_t^2/\sigma_t^2)$ with the variance-preserving constraint $\alpha_t^2 + \sigma_t^2 = 1$.
The KL divergence between the data-conditioned diffusion path and the learned reverse process can be bounded as~\citep{ho2020denoising,kingma2021variational}:
\begin{equation}
  \KL\!\Big[p(\vx_0|\vx) \,\Big\|\, p_\theta(\vx_0)\Big]
  \leq \E_{t\sim\mathcal{U}(0,1)}\!\left[-\frac{d\lambda(t)}{dt}\,\frac{\exp\lambda(t)}{2}\,w(\lambda_t)\,\|\vx - \hat{\vx}(\vx_t,\theta)\|^2\right]
  + \KL\!\Big[p(\vx_1|\vx) \,\Big\|\, \mathcal{N}(\bm{0},\bm{I})\Big],
  \label{eq:diff_kl}
\end{equation}
where $w(\lambda_t) = 1$ yields a tight bound, and $w(\lambda_t) = \sigmoid(\lambda_t - b)$ provides a perceptually motivated re-weighting.

\subsection{Unified Latents (UL)}
\label{sec:ul_background}

UL~\citep{heek2025ul} uses a deterministic encoder $\vz_{\text{clean}} = E_\theta(\vx)$ with fixed Gaussian noise added up to a minimum noise level defined by $\lambda_z(0) = 5$:
\begin{equation}
  \vz_0 = \alpha_z(0)\,\vz_{\text{clean}} + \sigma_z(0)\,\vep_z, \quad
  \alpha_z(0) = \sqrt{\sigmoid(+5)} \approx 1.0, \quad
  \sigma_z(0) = \sqrt{\sigmoid(-5)} \approx 0.08.
  \label{eq:ul_noise}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Prior loss (Equation~3 of UL).}
The KL penalty between the encoder distribution and the diffusion prior is:
\begin{equation}
  \KL\!\Big[p(\vz_0|\vx) \,\Big\|\, p_\theta(\vz_0)\Big]
  \leq \E_{t}\!\left[-\frac{d\lambda_z(t)}{dt}\,\frac{\exp\lambda_z(t)}{2}\,w(\lambda_z(t))\,\|\vz_{\text{clean}} - \hat{\vz}(\vz_t, \theta)\|^2\right]
  + \KL\!\Big[p(\vz_1|\vx) \,\Big\|\, \mathcal{N}(\bm{0},\bm{I})\Big],
  \label{eq:ul_prior}
\end{equation}
where $w(\lambda_z(t)) = 1$ for a tight bitrate bound.

\textbf{Decoder loss (Equation~4 of UL).}
The reconstruction term uses a diffusion decoder conditioned on $\vz_0$:
\begin{equation}
  -\log p_\theta(\vx|\vz_0) \leq \E_{t\sim\mathcal{U}(0,1)}\!\left[\frac{d\lambda_x(t)}{dt}\,\frac{\exp\lambda_x(t)}{2}\,w_x(\lambda_x(t))\,\|\vx - \hat{\vx}(\vx_t, \vz_0, \theta)\|^2\right],
  \label{eq:ul_decoder}
\end{equation}
with sigmoid weighting $w_x(\lambda_x(t)) = \sigmoid(\lambda_x(t) - b)$ and a loss factor $c_{\text{lf}} \in [1.3, 1.7]$ that up-weighs the decoder to prevent posterior collapse.

\subsection{Autoregressive Video-Action Models}
\label{sec:dreamzero_background}

DreamZero~\citep{dreamzero2025} introduced an autoregressive diffusion transformer that jointly generates video frames and robot actions.
Given a history of observations $\vo_{t-H+1:t}$, past actions $\va_{t-H:t-1}$, and a language instruction $\bm{l}$, the model jointly generates the current action $\va_t$ and the next observation $\vo_{t+1}$ (which results from executing $\va_t$):
\begin{equation}
  p_\theta(\va_t, \vo_{t+1} \mid \vo_{t-H+1:t}, \va_{t-H:t-1}, \bm{l}).
  \label{eq:dreamzero}
\end{equation}
This can be decomposed as video prediction followed by inverse dynamics: $p_\theta(\vo_{t+1} \mid \text{history}, \bm{l}) \times p_\theta(\va_t \mid \vo_{t+1}, \text{history}, \bm{l})$.
The generation is autoregressive across time steps: at step $t{-}1$ the model generated $(\va_{t-1}, \vo_t)$, and now at step $t$ it generates $(\va_t, \vo_{t+1})$, always producing the action that causes the next frame.
During closed-loop execution, predicted frames are replaced with ground-truth observations after each action, preventing error accumulation.
We adopt this autoregressive structure but operate in a learned latent space that is optimized for action-relevant content.

\section{Method: Action-Conditioned Unified Latents}
\label{sec:method}
%=====================================================================

We extend the UL framework from single-image encoding to \emph{history-conditioned joint action-observation dynamics}.
The central idea is to (1)~encode both observations and actions into learned latent spaces via observation encoder $E_\theta$ and action encoder $A_\theta$, and (2)~replace the unconditional prior $p_\theta(\vz_0)$ with a \emph{dynamics prior} $p_\theta(\vb_t, \vz_{t+1,0} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)$ that must jointly denoise the current action latent and the resulting future observation latent using a unified diffusion objective, conditioned on the interaction history and a language instruction.

\subsection{Problem Setup and Variables}
\label{sec:variables}

We consider a robotic agent that observes image frames, receives a language instruction, and executes actions in an autoregressive loop. We define the \emph{interaction history} at time $t$:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{H}_t = \big(\bm{l},\; \vo_{t-H+1}, \va_{t-H}, \vo_{t-H+2}, \va_{t-H+1}, \ldots, \vo_{t-1}, \va_{t-1}, \vo_t\big),
  \label{eq:history}
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item $\bm{l}$: a natural language instruction specifying the task (e.g., ``pick up the red block'').
  \item $\vo_k \in \R^{H_{\text{img}} \times W_{\text{img}} \times 3}$: RGB observation at time step $k$.
  \item $\va_k \in \R^{d_a}$: robot action executed at step $k$ (e.g., end-effector displacement + gripper state).
  \item $H$: history window length.
\end{itemize}

Note the autoregressive structure: the history contains observations $\vo_{t-H+1:t}$ and actions $\va_{t-H:t-1}$. At the previous time step, the model generated $(\va_{t-1}, \vo_t)$ as a pair---the action $\va_{t-1}$ \emph{caused} the transition to $\vo_t$. Now at time $t$, the model's task is to jointly generate:
\begin{equation}
  (\va_t, \vo_{t+1}) \sim p_\theta(\va_t, \vo_{t+1} \mid \mathcal{H}_t),
  \label{eq:joint_gen}
\end{equation}
where $\va_t$ is the action to execute now, and $\vo_{t+1}$ is the observation that \emph{results from executing} $\va_t$. The action $\va_t$ and the next frame $\vo_{t+1}$ are thus causally linked: the model must generate an action consistent with the predicted future.

\subsection{Latent Encoding and the Forward Process}
\label{sec:encoding}

\textbf{Observation encoder.}
The observation encoder $E_\theta$ compresses each observation into a deterministic latent:
\begin{equation}
  \vz_{k,\text{clean}} = E_\theta(\vo_k) \in \R^{h \times w \times c}, \quad k = t-H+1, \ldots, t, t+1,
  \label{eq:encode}
\end{equation}
with spatial dimensions $h \times w$ downsampled from $H_{\text{img}} \times W_{\text{img}}$ and $c$ latent channels.

\textbf{Action encoder.}
The action encoder $A_\theta$ maps each raw action into a deterministic action latent:
\begin{equation}
  \vb_k = A_\theta(\va_k) \in \R^{d_b}, \quad k = t-H, \ldots, t-1, t,
  \label{eq:action_encode}
\end{equation}
where $d_b$ is the action latent dimension. This encoding allows the action to be treated symmetrically with observation latents in the diffusion process.

The latent history is constructed from encoded observations and encoded actions:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{H}_t^z = \big(\bm{l},\; \vz_{t-H+1,\text{clean}}, \vb_{t-H}, \vz_{t-H+2,\text{clean}}, \vb_{t-H+1}, \ldots, \vb_{t-1}, \vz_{t,\text{clean}}\big).
  \label{eq:latent_history}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Forward process.}
Following UL, we add fixed Gaussian noise to the future clean latent and to the current action latent. For any diffusion time $\tau \in [0,1]$:
\begin{align}
  \vz_{t+1,\tau} &= \alpha_z(\tau)\,\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}} + \sigma_z(\tau)\,\vep_z, \quad \vep_z \sim \mathcal{N}(\bm{0}, \bm{I}),
  \label{eq:forward_latent} \\
  \vb_{t,\tau} &= \alpha_b(\tau)\,\vb_t + \sigma_b(\tau)\,\vep_b, \quad \vep_b \sim \mathcal{N}(\bm{0}, \bm{I}),
  \label{eq:forward_action}
\end{align}
where the log-SNR schedules $\lambda_z(\tau) = \log(\alpha_z^2(\tau)/\sigma_z^2(\tau))$ and $\lambda_b(\tau) = \log(\alpha_b^2(\tau)/\sigma_b^2(\tau))$ both satisfy the variance-preserving constraint.
The same diffusion time $\tau$ is shared between the observation and action latents, enabling joint denoising.

At $\tau = 0$ (minimum noise level), $\lambda_z(0) = 5$ gives:
\begin{equation}
  \alpha_z(0) = \sqrt{\sigmoid(5)} \approx 0.9967, \quad
  \sigma_z(0) = \sqrt{\sigmoid(-5)} \approx 0.0813.
  \label{eq:min_noise}
\end{equation}

\subsection{History-Conditioned Joint Dynamics Prior}
\label{sec:ac_prior}

This is the central contribution. In standard UL, the prior $\hat{\vz}(\vz_t, \theta)$ unconditionally denoises the latent.
We replace this with a \emph{joint dynamics prior} that conditions on the full interaction history $\mathcal{H}_t^z$ (including language instruction) and simultaneously denoises the current action latent and the resulting future observation latent:
\begin{equation}
  \big(\hat{\vb}_t,\; \hat{\vz}_{t+1,\text{clean}}\big) = \hat{f}_\theta^{\text{dyn}}\!\left(\vb_{t,\tau},\; \vz_{t+1,\tau},\; \mathcal{H}_t^z\right).
  \label{eq:dynamics_prior_pred}
\end{equation}

The prior jointly denoises:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Current action latent:} $\hat{\vb}_t \approx \vb_t$ --- the encoded action to take now, given the history and language goal.
  \item \textbf{Future observation latent:} $\hat{\vz}_{t+1,\text{clean}} \approx \vz_{t+1,\text{clean}}$ --- the observation that results from executing the action.
\end{enumerate}
Both are generated from noised inputs using the same diffusion process. The action latent and future observation latent are causally linked: the decoded action $D_\theta^a(\hat{\vb}_t)$ is the action that \emph{causes} the transition $\vo_t \to \vo_{t+1}$. The model must denoise both consistently.

\subsubsection{Derivation of the History-Conditioned Joint KL Bound}

We derive the AC-UL prior loss by applying the diffusion KL bound (\Cref{eq:diff_kl}) to the conditional distributions.
Since both observations and actions are encoded into latent spaces by deterministic encoders, the KL between the encoder distributions and the dynamics prior is over the \emph{joint} latent space:
\begin{align}
  &\KL\!\Big[p(\vz_{t+1,0}, \vb_{t,0} \mid \vo_{t+1}, \va_t, \mathcal{H}_t) \;\Big\|\; p_\theta(\vz_{t+1,0}, \vb_{t,0} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)\Big].
  \label{eq:joint_kl}
\end{align}

Since both encoders are deterministic, the encoder distribution factorizes as:
\begin{equation}
  p(\vz_{t+1,0}, \vb_{t,0} \mid \vo_{t+1}, \va_t, \mathcal{H}_t) = p(\vz_{t+1,0} \mid \vo_{t+1}) \cdot p(\vb_{t,0} \mid \va_t),
  \label{eq:encoder_factorize}
\end{equation}
where each factor is a narrow Gaussian centered at the deterministic encoder output (with minimum noise $\sigma(0) \approx 0.08$).

By independence of the two encoder distributions, the joint KL decomposes additively:
\begin{align}
  &\KL\!\Big[p(\vz_{t+1,0}, \vb_{t,0} \mid \vo_{t+1}, \va_t, \mathcal{H}_t) \;\Big\|\; p_\theta(\vz_{t+1,0}, \vb_{t,0} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)\Big] \nonumber \\
  &\geq \KL\!\Big[p(\vz_{t+1,0} \mid \vo_{t+1}) \;\Big\|\; p_\theta(\vz_{t+1,0} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)\Big] + \KL\!\Big[p(\vb_{t,0} \mid \va_t) \;\Big\|\; p_\theta(\vb_{t,0} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)\Big].
  \label{eq:joint_kl_decompose}
\end{align}

Each KL term is bounded via the diffusion decomposition.

\textbf{Observation latent loss:}
\begin{align}
  \loss_z^{\text{latent}}(\theta) &= \E_{\tau \sim \mathcal{U}(0,1)}\!\left[
    -\frac{d\lambda_z(\tau)}{d\tau}\,\frac{\exp\lambda_z(\tau)}{2}\,
    \left\|\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}} - \hat{\vz}_\theta^{\text{dyn}}\!\left(\vb_{t,\tau},\, \vz_{t+1,\tau},\, \mathcal{H}_t^z\right)\right\|^2
  \right] \nonumber \\
  &\quad + \KL\!\Big[p(\vz_{t+1,1} \mid \vo_{t+1}) \;\Big\|\; \mathcal{N}(\bm{0},\bm{I})\Big].
  \label{eq:ac_prior_loss}
\end{align}

\textbf{Action latent loss:}
\begin{align}
  \loss_b(\theta) &= \E_{\tau \sim \mathcal{U}(0,1)}\!\left[
    -\frac{d\lambda_b(\tau)}{d\tau}\,\frac{\exp\lambda_b(\tau)}{2}\,
    \left\|\vb_t - \hat{\vb}_\theta^{\text{dyn}}\!\left(\vb_{t,\tau},\, \vz_{t+1,\tau},\, \mathcal{H}_t^z\right)\right\|^2
  \right] \nonumber \\
  &\quad + \KL\!\Big[p(\vb_{t,1} \mid \va_t) \;\Big\|\; \mathcal{N}(\bm{0},\bm{I})\Big].
  \label{eq:action_latent_loss}
\end{align}

The complete prior loss combines both with a shared diffusion objective:
\begin{equation}
  \loss_z^{\text{joint}}(\theta) = \loss_z^{\text{latent}}(\theta) + \gamma \cdot \loss_b(\theta),
  \label{eq:joint_prior_loss}
\end{equation}
where $\gamma$ controls the relative weight of action denoising and both losses share the same sampled diffusion time $\tau$.

\subsubsection{Why History-Conditioned Joint Denoising Forces Physics-Grounded Encoding}

The loss is driven by two diffusion MSE terms: (i) the observation latent denoising error $\|\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}} - \hat{\vz}_{t+1}\|^2$ and (ii) the action latent denoising error $\|\vb_t - \hat{\vb}_t\|^2$.

Consider what happens if the observation encoder $E_\theta$ encodes information into $\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}}$ that is \emph{unpredictable} from the history $\mathcal{H}_t^z$:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Random textures/lighting:} If $E_\theta$ preserves random background variations, the dynamics prior cannot denoise these from the history (past latent frames, past latent actions, and the language goal do not determine future lighting). The observation latent MSE spikes.

  \item \textbf{Novel objects entering the scene:} Sudden appearance of objects not visible in the history is unpredictable. The encoder learns to down-weight such transients.

  \item \textbf{Action-caused geometry:} Gripper position, object displacement, and contact geometry \emph{are} predictable from the history via learned physics. The prior can reason: ``given the language goal `pick up red block', the history of gripper approach, and the denoised action latent $\hat{\vb}_t$ (close gripper), the next frame should show the gripper grasping the block.'' Encoding this geometry \emph{decreases} both MSE terms.
\end{enumerate}

The \emph{joint} denoising of $(\vb_t, \vz_{t+1})$ creates two complementary pressures:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Forward dynamics:} The prior must denoise $\vz_{t+1}$ --- what will happen after executing the action. This requires the observation latent to encode physical geometry that actions causally affect.
  \item \textbf{Inverse dynamics:} The prior must also denoise $\vb_t$ --- what action caused the transition $\vz_t \to \vz_{t+1}$. This prevents the observation encoder from collapsing to a trivially predictable but uninformative representation. Simultaneously, the action encoder $A_\theta$ must encode actions in a form that the prior can consistently denoise from the latent history.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Formal argument.}
The total information in the latent can be decomposed as:
\begin{equation}
  I(\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}}; \vo_{t+1}) = \underbrace{I(\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}}; \vo_{t+1} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)}_{\text{unpredictable info (penalized by } \loss_z^{\text{latent}}\text{)}} + \underbrace{I(\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}}; \mathcal{H}_t^z)}_{\text{history-predictable info (free)}}.
  \label{eq:info_decomp}
\end{equation}
The prior loss $\loss_z^{\text{latent}}$ upper-bounds the first term (see \Cref{sec:theory} for proof). The decoder loss $\loss_x$ incentivizes maximizing total information. The action latent loss $\loss_b$ ensures the latent captures the causal effect of actions (inverse dynamics signal) via the same diffusion objective. Together, they push both encoders toward representations that are maximally informative about action-predictable physics while discarding everything else.

\subsection{Diffusion Decoder for Future Frame Reconstruction}
\label{sec:decoder}

The decoder operates exactly as in standard UL but reconstructs the \emph{future} observation $\vo_{t+1}$. The noisy image is:
\begin{equation}
  \vo_{t+1,\tau} = \alpha_x(\tau)\,\vo_{t+1} + \sigma_x(\tau)\,\vep, \quad \vep \sim \mathcal{N}(\bm{0}, \bm{I}),
  \label{eq:forward_image}
\end{equation}
with its own log-SNR schedule $\lambda_x(\tau)$.

The decoder network $D_\theta = \hat{\vo}(\vo_{t+1,\tau}, \vz_{t+1,0}, \theta)$ conditions on both the noisy image and the slightly noisy future latent $\vz_{t+1,0}$. The reconstruction bound is:
\begin{equation}
  -\log p_\theta(\vo_{t+1} \mid \vz_{t+1,0}) \leq
  \E_{\tau \sim \mathcal{U}(0,1)}\!\left[
    \frac{d\lambda_x(\tau)}{d\tau}\,\frac{\exp\lambda_x(\tau)}{2}\,
    w_x(\lambda_x(\tau))\,
    \|\vo_{t+1} - \hat{\vo}(\vo_{t+1,\tau}, \vz_{t+1,0}, \theta)\|^2
  \right],
  \label{eq:ac_decoder_loss}
\end{equation}
with sigmoid weighting $w_x(\lambda_x(\tau)) = \sigmoid(\lambda_x(\tau) - b)$ and loss factor $c_{\text{lf}} \in [1.3, 1.7]$.

\subsection{Action Decoder}
\label{sec:action_decoder}

To recover the raw action $\va_t \in \R^{d_a}$ from the denoised action latent $\hat{\vb}_t$, we use a lightweight action decoder:
\begin{equation}
  \hat{\va}_t = D_\theta^a(\hat{\vb}_t) \in \R^{d_a}.
  \label{eq:action_decode}
\end{equation}
The action decoder is a 2-layer MLP trained with an auxiliary reconstruction loss:
\begin{equation}
  \loss_a^{\text{recon}}(\theta) = \|\va_t - D_\theta^a(\vb_t)\|^2.
  \label{eq:action_recon_loss}
\end{equation}
This loss is applied to the clean (non-noised) action latent $\vb_t$ and ensures that the action encoder-decoder pair faithfully represents the action space.

\subsection{Complete Stage 1 Objective}
\label{sec:stage1_objective}

The total per-transition loss combines the joint prior loss, the decoder loss, and the action reconstruction loss:
\begin{equation}
  \loss_{\text{S1}}(\theta) = \underbrace{\loss_z^{\text{latent}}(\theta) + \gamma \cdot \loss_b(\theta)}_{\text{joint dynamics prior (diffusion)}} + c_{\text{lf}} \cdot \underbrace{\loss_x(\theta)}_{\text{decoder}} + \underbrace{\loss_a^{\text{recon}}(\theta)}_{\text{action reconstruction}}.
  \label{eq:total_loss}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Interpretation of each term:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item $\loss_z^{\text{latent}}$: Diffusion denoising loss for the observation latent. Penalizes unpredictable information in the future latent given the history. Forces the observation encoder to discard action-irrelevant content.
  \item $\loss_b$: Diffusion denoising loss for the action latent. Uses the same diffusion objective as $\loss_z^{\text{latent}}$. Requires the latent history to contain enough dynamics structure that the action can be denoised. Prevents trivial latent collapse.
  \item $\loss_x$: Observation reconstruction loss. Rewards information that aids reconstruction. Ensures the latent retains fine geometric detail (e.g., gripper-object distance).
  \item $\loss_a^{\text{recon}}$: Action reconstruction loss. Ensures the action encoder-decoder pair faithfully represents actions.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Stage 1 Training Algorithm}
\label{sec:stage1_algo}

The complete Stage~1 training procedure is given in \Cref{alg:stage1}.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{Stage 1: Training AC-UL with Joint Dynamics Prior}
\label{alg:stage1}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\REQUIRE Dataset of trajectories $\{(\bm{l}, \vo_0, \va_0, \vo_1, \va_1, \ldots)\}$, history length $H$
\STATE Sample window: $(\bm{l}, \vo_{t-H+1:t+1}, \va_{t-H:t}) \sim \mathcal{D}$
\STATE \textbf{// Encode observations and actions}
\STATE Encode observations: $\vz_{k,\text{clean}} = E_\theta(\vo_k)$ for $k = t{-}H{+}1, \ldots, t{+}1$
\STATE Encode actions: $\vb_k = A_\theta(\va_k)$ for $k = t{-}H, \ldots, t$
\STATE Construct latent history: $\mathcal{H}_t^z = (\bm{l}, \vz_{t-H+1,\text{clean}}, \vb_{t-H}, \ldots, \vb_{t-1}, \vz_{t,\text{clean}})$
\STATE \textbf{// Joint dynamics prior loss (shared diffusion objective)}
\STATE Sample $\tau \sim \mathcal{U}(0,1)$, $\vep_z \sim \mathcal{N}(\bm{0}, \bm{I})$, $\vep_b \sim \mathcal{N}(\bm{0}, \bm{I})$
\STATE $\vz_{t+1,\tau} = \alpha_z(\tau)\,\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}} + \sigma_z(\tau)\,\vep_z$
\STATE $\vb_{t,\tau} = \alpha_b(\tau)\,\vb_t + \sigma_b(\tau)\,\vep_b$
\STATE $(\hat{\vz}_{t+1}, \hat{\vb}_t) = \hat{f}_\theta^{\text{dyn}}(\vb_{t,\tau}, \vz_{t+1,\tau}, \mathcal{H}_t^z)$
\STATE $\loss_z^{\text{latent}} = -\frac{d\lambda_z(\tau)}{d\tau}\,\frac{\exp\lambda_z(\tau)}{2}\,\|\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}} - \hat{\vz}_{t+1}\|^2 + \KL[p(\vz_{t+1,1}|\vo_{t+1})\|\mathcal{N}(\bm{0},\bm{I})]$
\STATE $\loss_b = -\frac{d\lambda_b(\tau)}{d\tau}\,\frac{\exp\lambda_b(\tau)}{2}\,\|\vb_t - \hat{\vb}_t\|^2 + \KL[p(\vb_{t,1}|\va_t)\|\mathcal{N}(\bm{0},\bm{I})]$
\STATE \textbf{// Action reconstruction loss}
\STATE $\loss_a^{\text{recon}} = \|\va_t - D_\theta^a(\vb_t)\|^2$
\STATE \textbf{// Decoder loss}
\STATE Sample $\tau' \sim \mathcal{U}(0,1)$, $\vep' \sim \mathcal{N}(\bm{0}, \bm{I})$, $\vep_z' \sim \mathcal{N}(\bm{0}, \bm{I})$
\STATE $\vz_{t+1,0} = \alpha_z(0)\,\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}} + \sigma_z(0)\,\vep_z'$
\STATE $\vo_{t+1,\tau'} = \alpha_x(\tau')\,\vo_{t+1} + \sigma_x(\tau')\,\vep'$
\STATE $\loss_x = \frac{d\lambda_x(\tau')}{d\tau'}\,\frac{\exp\lambda_x(\tau')}{2}\,w_x(\lambda_x(\tau'))\,\|\vo_{t+1} - \hat{\vo}(\vo_{t+1,\tau'}, \vz_{t+1,0}, \theta)\|^2$
\STATE \textbf{// Combined loss}
\STATE $\loss_{\text{S1}} = \loss_z^{\text{latent}} + \gamma \cdot \loss_b + c_{\text{lf}} \cdot \loss_x + \loss_a^{\text{recon}}$
\STATE Update $\theta$ by gradient descent on $\loss_{\text{S1}}$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Architecture Details}
\label{sec:architecture}

\textbf{Encoder $E_\theta$.}
A ResNet-style convolutional encoder with channel progression $[128, 256, 512, 512]$, using 2 residual blocks per downsampling stage and 3 blocks in the final stage. The encoder takes $224 \times 224$ RGB images and produces $14 \times 14 \times c$ latents with $c = 32$ channels, yielding $16\times$ spatial downsampling.

\textbf{Action encoder $A_\theta$.}
A 3-layer MLP with hidden dimension 256 and ReLU activations, mapping raw actions $\va_k \in \R^{d_a}$ to action latents $\vb_k \in \R^{d_b}$ with $d_b = 64$.

\textbf{Action decoder $D_\theta^a$.}
A 2-layer MLP that maps denoised action latents $\hat{\vb}_t \in \R^{d_b}$ back to raw actions $\hat{\va}_t \in \R^{d_a}$.

\textbf{Joint dynamics prior $\hat{f}_\theta^{\text{dyn}}$.}
A causal Vision Transformer (ViT) with 12 blocks and 768 hidden dimensions. The input sequence is constructed as language tokens followed by interleaved observation-action latent history, plus the noised action and observation latents to be denoised:
\begin{equation}
  \big[\text{text\_enc}(\bm{l}),\; \text{patchify}(\vz_{t-H+1,\text{clean}}),\; \text{mlp}(\vb_{t-H}),\; \ldots,\; \text{mlp}(\vb_{t-1}),\; \text{patchify}(\vz_{t,\text{clean}}),\; \text{mlp}(\vb_{t,\tau}),\; \text{patchify}(\vz_{t+1,\tau})\big].
  \label{eq:prior_input}
\end{equation}
The language instruction $\bm{l}$ is encoded by a frozen text encoder into a sequence of tokens prepended to the history. Each observation latent $\vz_{k,\text{clean}}$ is patchified into spatial tokens; each action latent $\vb_k$ is projected through an MLP into a single token. The noised action latent $\vb_{t,\tau}$ and noised observation latent $\vz_{t+1,\tau}$ are appended as the denoising targets. The diffusion timestep $\tau$ is encoded via sinusoidal embeddings and added to the noisy target tokens.

The model uses \emph{frame-level causal attention}: tokens at time $k$ can attend to all tokens at times $\leq k$ but not future times (following DreamZero). The model has two output heads:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Observation latent head:} Linear projection from the output tokens at the $\vz_{t+1,\tau}$ positions $\to \hat{\vz}_{t+1,\text{clean}} \in \R^{h \times w \times c}$.
  \item \textbf{Action latent head:} Linear projection from the output tokens at the $\vb_{t,\tau}$ position $\to \hat{\vb}_t \in \R^{d_b}$.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Diffusion decoder $D_\theta$.}
A UViT architecture with convolutional down/up-sampling stages $[128, 256, 512]$ and a central transformer with 8 blocks and 1024 channels. The decoder takes the noisy image $\vo_{t+1,\tau}$ and conditions on $\vz_{t+1,0}$ via adaptive layer normalization (AdaLN).

%=====================================================================
\section{Stage 2: Autoregressive Diffusion Transformer on Frozen Latents}
\label{sec:stage2}
%=====================================================================

Once Stage~1 converges, we freeze the observation encoder $E_\theta$, action encoder $A_\theta$, observation decoder $D_\theta$, and action decoder $D_\theta^a$. The frozen encoders provide a \emph{physics-grounded vocabulary} that has been optimized to contain only action-predictable geometry.

For Stage~2, we train a larger autoregressive diffusion transformer that operates entirely in the frozen latent space, following the DreamZero architecture but on AC-UL latents instead of raw video and raw actions.

\subsection{Latent Trajectory Extraction}

Given a trajectory $(\vo_0, \va_0, \vo_1, \va_1, \ldots, \vo_T)$, we extract clean latents for both observations and actions:
\begin{align}
  \vz_{k,\text{clean}} &= E_\theta(\vo_k), \quad k = 0, 1, \ldots, T, \label{eq:latent_extract} \\
  \vb_k &= A_\theta(\va_k), \quad k = 0, 1, \ldots, T-1. \label{eq:action_latent_extract}
\end{align}

The trajectory in latent space becomes:
\begin{equation}
  (\vz_{0,\text{clean}}, \vb_0, \vz_{1,\text{clean}}, \vb_1, \ldots, \vz_{T,\text{clean}}).
  \label{eq:latent_trajectory}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Joint Video-Action Generation in Latent Space}

The Stage~2 model $\mathcal{T}_\phi$ is a diffusion transformer that takes the latent history and jointly generates the future latent and current action. We follow a chunk-wise autoregressive scheme inspired by DreamZero.

\textbf{Chunk definition.}
The trajectory is divided into chunks of $K$ latent frames. Each chunk $k$ contains interleaved observation and action latents:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{C}_k = \big(\vz_{kK+1,\text{clean}}, \vb_{kK}, \vz_{kK+2,\text{clean}}, \vb_{kK+1}, \ldots, \vz_{(k+1)K,\text{clean}}, \vb_{(k+1)K-1}\big).
  \label{eq:chunk}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Flow-matching training.}
For each chunk, we sample noise and construct interpolants for both the observation latents and action latents:
\begin{align}
  \vz_{kK+j,\tau_k} &= (1-\tau_k)\,\vz_{kK+j,\text{clean}} + \tau_k\,\vep_z^{(j)}, \label{eq:flow_latent} \\
  \vb_{kK+j-1,\tau_k} &= (1-\tau_k)\,\vb_{kK+j-1} + \tau_k\,\vep_b^{(j)}, \label{eq:flow_action}
\end{align}
where $\tau_k \sim \mathcal{U}(0,1)$ is sampled independently per chunk. The model predicts the velocity field:
\begin{equation}
  \loss_{\text{S2}} = \E\!\left[\frac{1}{K}\sum_{k} w(\tau_k)\,\left\|
    \mathcal{T}_\phi\!\left([\vz_{\tau_k}^k, \vb_{\tau_k}^k]; \mathcal{C}_{<k}, \bm{l}, \bm{s}, \tau_k\right)
    - [\vz_{\text{clean}}^k - \vep_z^k,\; \vb^k - \vep_b^k]
  \right\|^2\right],
  \label{eq:stage2_flow}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{C}_{<k}$ denotes the clean context from previous chunks, $\bm{l}$ is the language instruction embedding, and $\bm{s}$ is the proprioceptive state. Note that both observation and action latents are denoised with the same flow-matching objective.

\textbf{Teacher forcing.}
During training, the model denoises the current chunk conditioned on \emph{clean} previous chunks via attention masking. This mirrors DreamZero's training but operates on compact AC-UL latents rather than high-dimensional video tokens and raw actions.

\subsection{Closed-Loop Inference}
\label{sec:inference}

During deployment, the model operates in a closed loop. At each step $t$, the history $\mathcal{H}_t^z$ contains the language instruction, past observation latents $\vz_{t-H+1:t,\text{clean}}$, and past action latents $\vb_{t-H:t-1}$:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Encode:} $\vz_{t,\text{clean}} = E_\theta(\vo_t)$ --- encode the current observation with the frozen observation encoder.
  \item \textbf{Generate:} $(\hat{\vb}_t, \hat{\vz}_{t+1,\text{clean}}) = \mathcal{T}_\phi(\mathcal{H}_t^z)$ --- jointly denoise the action latent and observation latent via iterative denoising from noise.
  \item \textbf{Decode action:} $\hat{\va}_t = D_\theta^a(\hat{\vb}_t)$ --- decode the denoised action latent to a raw action using the frozen action decoder.
  \item \textbf{Execute:} Apply $\hat{\va}_t$ to the robot. This action \emph{causes} the transition to the next state.
  \item \textbf{Observe:} Receive the new observation $\vo_{t+1}$ (the actual result of $\hat{\va}_t$).
  \item \textbf{Replace:} Compute $\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}} = E_\theta(\vo_{t+1})$ and replace $\hat{\vz}_{t+1,\text{clean}}$ in the KV cache. Compute $\vb_t = A_\theta(\hat{\va}_t)$ for the history. This prevents error accumulation.
  \item \textbf{Advance:} Shift the history window: $\vb_t$ becomes part of the past action latents, $\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}}$ becomes the latest observation latent. Go to step 2 with $t \leftarrow t+1$.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Planning via Latent Rollouts}
\label{sec:planning}

For goal-conditioned tasks, the world model head enables planning without a separate planner. Given a goal observation $\vo_g$, we:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item Encode the goal: $\vz_{g,\text{clean}} = E_\theta(\vo_g)$.
  \item Use the joint model to autoregressively generate action-latent rollouts \emph{without} ground-truth replacement:
  \begin{equation}
    (\hat{\vb}_t, \hat{\vz}_{t+1}) \to (\hat{\vb}_{t+1}, \hat{\vz}_{t+2}) \to \cdots \to (\hat{\vb}_{t+P-1}, \hat{\vz}_{t+P}).
    \label{eq:open_loop_rollout}
  \end{equation}
  At each step, the predicted $\hat{\vz}$ and $\hat{\vb}$ are fed back as context for the next step.
  \item Score each rollout by the terminal distance: $d = \|\hat{\vz}_{t+P,\text{clean}} - \vz_{g,\text{clean}}\|^2$.
  \item Use CEM or best-of-$N$ sampling to select the best action sequence.
  \item Execute the first action and replan (receding horizon).
\end{enumerate}

Because the latents encode only action-relevant geometry, the planning cost function directly measures progress toward the goal in terms of physical state change.

\section{Theoretical Analysis}
\label{sec:theory}
%=====================================================================

\subsection{AC-UL as a Variational Bound on Joint Transition Likelihood}

\begin{proposition}[AC-UL Joint Bound]
The AC-UL Stage~1 loss provides an upper bound on the negative log-likelihood of the joint action-observation transition:
\begin{equation}
  -\log p_\theta(\va_t, \vo_{t+1} \mid \mathcal{H}_t) \leq \loss_z^{\text{latent}}(\theta) + \gamma \cdot \loss_b(\theta) + c_{\text{lf}} \cdot \loss_x(\theta) + \loss_a^{\text{recon}}(\theta),
  \label{eq:acul_bound}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{H}_t = (\bm{l}, \vo_{t-H+1:t}, \va_{t-H:t-1})$ includes the language instruction.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Starting from the ELBO applied to the conditional model with joint latent $(\vz_{t+1,0}, \vb_{t,0})$:
\begin{align}
  -\log p_\theta(\va_t, \vo_{t+1} \mid \mathcal{H}_t)
  &\leq \E_{\vz_{t+1,0}, \vb_{t,0}}\!\Big[-\log p_\theta(\vo_{t+1} \mid \vz_{t+1,0})\Big] \nonumber \\
  &\quad + \KL\!\Big[p(\vz_{t+1,0} \mid \vo_{t+1}) \;\Big\|\; p_\theta(\vz_{t+1,0} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)\Big] \nonumber \\
  &\quad + \KL\!\Big[p(\vb_{t,0} \mid \va_t) \;\Big\|\; p_\theta(\vb_{t,0} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)\Big] \nonumber \\
  &\quad - \E_{\vb_{t,0}}\!\Big[\log p_\theta(\va_t \mid \vb_{t,0})\Big].
  \label{eq:proof_step}
\end{align}
Applying the diffusion bounds (\Cref{eq:ac_prior_loss,eq:action_latent_loss,eq:ac_decoder_loss}) to the first three terms, and bounding $-\log p_\theta(\va_t \mid \vb_{t,0}) \leq \|\va_t - D_\theta^a(\vb_t)\|^2$ (Gaussian action likelihood) for the fourth term, yields the result.
The loss factor $c_{\text{lf}}$ loosens the bound but improves optimization by preventing posterior collapse.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Information-Theoretic Interpretation}

\textbf{Rate (prior loss).}
The prior loss $\loss_z^{\text{latent}}$ upper-bounds the mutual information between the encoder output and the data, \emph{beyond what is predictable from the history}:
\begin{equation}
  \loss_z^{\text{latent}} \geq I(\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}}; \vo_{t+1} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z).
  \label{eq:rate_bound}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Action regularization.}
The action latent loss $\loss_b$ provides an additional inductive bias via the same diffusion objective. The prior must denoise the action latent from the latent history. This means the latent space must encode the \emph{causal effect} of the action on the scene:
\begin{equation}
  \loss_b \geq I(\vb_t; \va_t \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z),
  \label{eq:action_info}
\end{equation}
so minimizing $\loss_b$ pushes the action encoder to encode only the action information that is predictable from the history, while ensuring the latent transition $\vz_t \to \vz_{t+1}$ remains informative about the action.

\textbf{Equilibrium.}
At convergence, both encoders achieve an optimal trade-off: they maximize history-predictable, action-relevant information while minimizing unpredictable content:
\begin{equation}
  \vz_{t+1,\text{clean}}^* = \arg\min_{\vz} \Big[\underbrace{I(\vz; \vo_{t+1} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)}_{\text{obs.\ rate}} + c_{\text{lf}} \cdot \underbrace{D(\vo_{t+1}, \hat{\vo}_\theta)}_{\text{distortion}} - \gamma \cdot \underbrace{I(\vb_t; \vz \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)}_{\text{action-obs coupling}}\Big].
  \label{eq:rd_tradeoff}
\end{equation}

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We evaluate AC-UL across three experimental settings: (1)~closed-loop planning via latent rollouts, (2)~imitation learning with joint world-action modeling, and (3)~real-world video prediction.

\subsection{Datasets and Benchmarks}
\label{sec:datasets}

\textbf{CALVIN}~\citep{mees2022calvin}.
A simulated long-horizon manipulation benchmark with 34 tasks (e.g., open drawer, push block, rotate lever). Provides RGB observations ($200 \times 200$), 7-DoF Franka Panda actions, and language instructions. We use the ABCD$\to$D split with ${\sim}600$K transitions and ${\sim}24$ hours of play data.

\textbf{LIBERO}~\citep{liu2024libero}.
A suite of 130 manipulation tasks across 5 task suites (Spatial, Object, Goal, Long, 90), featuring diverse spatial relations and long-horizon reasoning. Each task has 50 demonstrations with $128 \times 128$ RGB and 7-DoF actions.

\textbf{DROID}~\citep{khazatsky2024droid}.
A large-scale real-world dataset with ${\sim}76$K trajectories across 564 scenes on Franka Panda robots. Provides multi-view RGB ($320 \times 240$), 7-DoF actions, and language annotations. We use this for video prediction and cross-embodiment evaluation.

\textbf{Bridge-v2}~\citep{walke2023bridgedata}.
A real-world dataset with ${\sim}60$K trajectories on a WidowX robot with 7-DoF actions across kitchen tasks. Used for cross-embodiment transfer experiments.

\subsection{Baselines}
\label{sec:baselines}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{DreamerV3}~\citep{hafner2023dreamerv3}: Categorical latent world model with actor-critic planning.
  \item \textbf{DreamZero}~\citep{dreamzero2025}: Autoregressive diffusion transformer for joint video-action generation in pixel space. Direct comparison for the benefit of AC-UL latents vs.\ raw pixels.
  \item \textbf{DINO-WM}~\citep{zhou2024dinowm}: Frozen DINOv2 features with ViT transition model and CEM planning.
  \item \textbf{FLARE}~\citep{nvidia2025flare}: Diffusion transformer VLA with future latent alignment using SigLIP-2 features.
  \item \textbf{TD-MPC2}~\citep{hansen2024tdmpc2}: Decoder-free latent dynamics model with temporal difference learning.
  \item \textbf{IRIS}~\citep{micheli2023transformers}: Discrete autoencoder + GPT-style world model.
  \item \textbf{SD-Latent WM}: Stable Diffusion VAE encoder (frozen) + ViT dynamics model.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Experiment 3: Real-World Video-Action Prediction}
\label{sec:exp_video}

\subsubsection{Setup}

We evaluate the quality of joint video-action prediction on the DROID dataset. We train Stage~1 on DROID training split, then evaluate:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Latent prediction accuracy:} MSE between predicted and ground-truth latents over multi-step rollouts (1, 5, 10, 20 steps).
  \item \textbf{Action prediction accuracy:} MSE between predicted and ground-truth actions.
  \item \textbf{Decoded video quality:} FVD, LPIPS, SSIM, PSNR of decoded predictions vs.\ ground truth.

\section{Analysis and Discussion}
\label{sec:analysis}
%=====================================================================

\subsection{What Do AC-UL Latents Encode?}

To verify that AC-UL latents focus on action-relevant geometry, we analyze:

\textbf{Latent sensitivity to actions.}
We compute the Jacobian $\partial \vz_{t+1,\text{clean}} / \partial \va_t$ and visualize which spatial positions are most sensitive to action perturbations. We expect high sensitivity near the gripper and manipulated objects.

\textbf{Latent change vs.\ action magnitude.}
We measure the Pearson correlation between $\|\Delta \vz_t\|$ and $\|\va_t\|$. High correlation confirms that latent dynamics are action-driven.

\textbf{Closed-loop vs.\ open-loop rollouts.}
Because AC-UL latents are compact and action-focused, we expect open-loop rollouts (without ground-truth replacement) to degrade more gracefully than pixel-space rollouts, as there is less irrelevant variation to compound.

\subsection{Comparison with DreamZero}

AC-UL and DreamZero share the autoregressive joint video-action generation paradigm but differ fundamentally in representation:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{DreamZero} operates on VAE-compressed video tokens. The VAE is trained for generic visual reconstruction, not action-relevant content.
  \item \textbf{AC-UL} operates on latents specifically optimized to discard action-irrelevant information. This should yield (a) more compact sequences (fewer tokens per frame), (b) better long-horizon rollout fidelity, and (c) faster inference.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Comparison with FLARE}

FLARE adds future latent prediction as an auxiliary loss using frozen SigLIP-2 features. AC-UL differs:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item FLARE's targets (SigLIP-2) encode general visual semantics, not action-predictable physics.
  \item AC-UL's latents are \emph{trained} to contain only action-predictable geometry.
  \item AC-UL jointly generates observations and actions; FLARE generates only actions with future features as auxiliary.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Limitations}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Two-stage training:} Stage~1 requires training five components simultaneously (observation encoder, action encoder, dynamics prior, observation decoder, action decoder).
  \item \textbf{Deterministic encoders:} Inherently stochastic environments cannot be fully captured by the deterministic observation and action encoders.
  \item \textbf{Fixed encoders at Stage~2:} If Stage~1 training is suboptimal, Stage~2 inherits these limitations.
  \item \textbf{Real-time inference:} The observation diffusion decoder adds latency for visualization; however, the action can be extracted by decoding only the lightweight action latent.
\end{itemize}

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}
%=====================================================================

\textbf{World models for robotics.}
Early work used recurrent state-space models~\citep{ha2018world,hafner2019dream}. DreamerV3~\citep{hafner2023dreamerv3} uses categorical latents. IRIS~\citep{micheli2023transformers} tokenizes observations with a discrete autoencoder. TD-MPC2~\citep{hansen2024tdmpc2} learns latent dynamics without a decoder. DreamZero~\citep{dreamzero2025} introduced autoregressive joint video-action generation at scale.

\textbf{Pretrained features for world models.}
DINO-WM~\citep{zhou2024dinowm} uses frozen DINOv2 patch features. R3M~\citep{nair2022r3m} and VIP~\citep{ma2023vip} learn visual representations for robotics. V-JEPA 2 uses self-supervised video features for zero-shot manipulation.

\textbf{Latent representations for generation.}
Latent Diffusion Models~\citep{rombach2022high} learn autoencoders for diffusion. Unified Latents~\citep{heek2025ul} jointly trains encoders and diffusion priors. Our work extends UL to action-conditioned dynamics.

\textbf{Vision-Language-Action models.}
RT-2~\citep{brohan2023rt2}, OpenVLA~\citep{kim2024openvla}, and GR00T N1~\citep{nvidia2025groot} fine-tune VLMs for action prediction. FLARE~\citep{nvidia2025flare} adds future latent alignment. $\pi_0$~\citep{pi02024} uses flow matching for action generation. Our Stage~2 builds on this paradigm but uses physics-grounded latents.

\textbf{Joint video-action generation.}
DreamZero~\citep{dreamzero2025} and UniSim~\citep{yang2024unisim} jointly generate video and actions. UWM~\citep{zhu2025uwm} couples video and action diffusion. Our work provides a principled latent space for such joint generation.

%=====================================================================
\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
%=====================================================================

We introduced Action-Conditioned Unified Latents (AC-UL), a framework for learning joint observation-action representations optimized to encode only the physical geometry predictable from the history of interactions and language instructions.
By extending the Unified Latents variational objective with learned action encoding and a history-conditioned joint dynamics prior that simultaneously denoises the current action latent and the resulting future observation latent using a unified diffusion objective, we create an information bottleneck that discards unpredictable content while retaining action-relevant structure.

Our formulation follows the autoregressive paradigm of DreamZero---where each step generates an action and the frame it causes---but operates in principled latent spaces for \emph{both} observations and actions.
Stage~1 trains the observation encoder, action encoder, dynamics prior, and decoders via the AC-UL objective, and Stage~2 trains an autoregressive diffusion transformer on frozen latents for joint action-observation generation in closed loop.

We believe AC-UL opens promising directions: scaling Stage~2 to foundation-model sizes, incorporating action-free video for broader pretraining, and extending to multi-step chunk-wise generation for real-time control.

\subsection{Full Derivation of the History-Conditioned Joint Prior Bound}
\label{app:prior_derivation}

\textbf{Step 1: ELBO for joint conditional generation.}
We model the joint distribution $p_\theta(\va_t, \vo_{t+1} \mid \mathcal{H}_t)$, where $\mathcal{H}_t = (\bm{l}, \vo_{t-H+1:t}, \va_{t-H:t-1})$ includes the language instruction. Introducing joint latent variables $(\vz_{t+1,0}, \vb_{t,0})$---the observation latent and action latent respectively:
\begin{align}
  -\log p_\theta(\vo_{t+1}, \va_t \mid \mathcal{H}_t)
  &\leq -\E_{q(\vz_{t+1,0} | \vo_{t+1})\, q(\vb_{t,0} | \va_t)}\!\left[\log p_\theta(\vo_{t+1} | \vz_{t+1,0}) + \log p_\theta(\va_t | \vb_{t,0})\right] \nonumber \\
  &\quad + \KL\!\left[q(\vz_{t+1,0} | \vo_{t+1}) \,\|\, p_\theta(\vz_{t+1,0} | \mathcal{H}_t^z)\right] \nonumber \\
  &\quad + \KL\!\left[q(\vb_{t,0} | \va_t) \,\|\, p_\theta(\vb_{t,0} | \mathcal{H}_t^z)\right],
  \label{eq:app_joint_elbo}
\end{align}
where $q(\vz_{t+1,0} | \vo_{t+1}) = \mathcal{N}(\alpha_z(0)\, E_\theta(\vo_{t+1}),\, \sigma_z^2(0)\, \bm{I})$ and $q(\vb_{t,0} | \va_t) = \mathcal{N}(\alpha_b(0)\, A_\theta(\va_t),\, \sigma_b^2(0)\, \bm{I})$.

\textbf{Step 2: Bounding the observation latent KL with diffusion.}
The prior $p_\theta(\vz_{t+1,0} | \mathcal{H}_t^z)$ is a history-conditioned diffusion model. Augmenting with the full path:
\begin{align}
  &\KL\!\left[q(\vz_{t+1,0} | \vo_{t+1}) \,\|\, p_\theta(\vz_{t+1,0} | \mathcal{H}_t^z)\right] \nonumber \\
  &\leq \KL\!\left[q(\vz_{t+1,0:1} | \vo_{t+1}) \,\|\, p_\theta(\vz_{t+1,0:1} | \mathcal{H}_t^z)\right].
  \label{eq:app_chain}
\end{align}

\textbf{Step 3: Decomposing the observation path KL.}
Following \citet{kingma2021variational}:
\begin{align}
  &\KL\!\left[q(\vz_{t+1,0:1} | \vo_{t+1}) \,\|\, p_\theta(\vz_{t+1,0:1} | \mathcal{H}_t^z)\right] \nonumber \\
  &= \int_0^1 \E_{q(\vz_{t+1,\tau} | \vo_{t+1})}\!\left[-\frac{d\lambda_z(\tau)}{d\tau}\, \frac{\exp\lambda_z(\tau)}{2}\, \|\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}} - \hat{\vz}_\theta^{\text{dyn}}(\vb_{t,\tau}, \vz_{t+1,\tau}, \mathcal{H}_t^z)\|^2\right] d\tau \nonumber \\
  &\quad + \KL\!\left[q(\vz_{t+1,1} | \vo_{t+1}) \,\|\, \mathcal{N}(\bm{0}, \bm{I})\right].
  \label{eq:app_decompose}
\end{align}

\textbf{Step 4: Bounding the action latent KL with diffusion.}
The action latent follows the same diffusion process:
\begin{align}
  &\KL\!\left[q(\vb_{t,0} | \va_t) \,\|\, p_\theta(\vb_{t,0} | \mathcal{H}_t^z)\right] \nonumber \\
  &\leq \int_0^1 \E_{q(\vb_{t,\tau} | \va_t)}\!\left[-\frac{d\lambda_b(\tau)}{d\tau}\, \frac{\exp\lambda_b(\tau)}{2}\, \|\vb_t - \hat{\vb}_\theta^{\text{dyn}}(\vb_{t,\tau}, \vz_{t+1,\tau}, \mathcal{H}_t^z)\|^2\right] d\tau \nonumber \\
  &\quad + \KL\!\left[q(\vb_{t,1} | \va_t) \,\|\, \mathcal{N}(\bm{0}, \bm{I})\right].
  \label{eq:app_action_diffusion}
\end{align}

The conditioning on $\mathcal{H}_t^z$ is the key difference from standard UL: the prior uses the full interaction history---language instruction, past observation latents, and past action latents---to jointly denoise both the action and observation latents. The denoised action latent $\hat{\vb}_t$ represents the action that \emph{causes} the transition to $\vo_{t+1}$, so the model must denoise both consistently.

\textbf{Step 5: Action reconstruction term.}
The raw action is recovered from the action latent via the action decoder:
\begin{align}
  -\log p_\theta(\va_t | \vb_{t,0}) &\propto \|\va_t - D_\theta^a(\vb_t)\|^2 = \loss_a^{\text{recon}}.
  \label{eq:app_action_recon}
\end{align}

\textbf{Step 6: Combining all terms.}
The complete bound is:
\begin{align}
  -\log p_\theta(\vo_{t+1}, \va_t | \mathcal{H}_t) &\leq \underbrace{\loss_z^{\text{latent}}(\theta)}_{\text{obs.\ latent KL bound}} + \gamma \cdot \underbrace{\loss_b(\theta)}_{\text{action latent KL bound}} + c_{\text{lf}} \cdot \underbrace{\loss_x(\theta)}_{\text{decoder bound}} + \underbrace{\loss_a^{\text{recon}}(\theta)}_{\text{action reconstruction}}.
  \label{eq:app_total}
\end{align}

\subsection{Information-Theoretic Analysis}
\label{app:info_theory}

\textbf{Claim.}
The AC-UL prior loss upper-bounds $I(\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}}; \vo_{t+1} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)$.

\textbf{Proof.}
The KL in the ELBO satisfies:
\begin{align}
  \E_{p(\mathcal{H}_t)}\!\left[\KL\!\left[q(\vz_{t+1,0} | \vo_{t+1}) \,\|\, p_\theta(\vz_{t+1,0} | \mathcal{H}_t^z)\right]\right]
  &\geq I(\vz_{t+1,0}; \vo_{t+1} | \mathcal{H}_t^z).
\end{align}
When the prior is optimal ($p_\theta = p^*$), equality holds. Since $\loss_z^{\text{latent}}$ upper-bounds the KL, we have $\loss_z^{\text{latent}} \geq I(\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}}; \vo_{t+1} | \mathcal{H}_t^z)$.

\textbf{Role of action latent diffusion loss.}
The action latent loss $\loss_b$ provides an additional constraint via the same diffusion framework. The KL bound gives:
\begin{equation}
  \loss_b \geq I(\vb_t; \va_t \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z).
\end{equation}
At optimality, the joint denoising of $(\vb_t, \vz_{t+1})$ ensures that the action latent and observation latent are mutually informative:
\begin{equation}
  I(\vb_t; \vz_{t+1,\text{clean}} | \mathcal{H}_t^z) \geq I(\va_t; \vo_{t+1} | \mathcal{H}_t^z) - I(\va_t; \vo_{t+1} | \vz_{t+1,\text{clean}}, \mathcal{H}_t^z).
\end{equation}
The joint diffusion loss pushes $\vz_{t+1,\text{clean}}$ to be a \emph{sufficient statistic} for the action given the history, meaning $I(\va_t; \vo_{t+1} | \vz_{t+1,\text{clean}}, \mathcal{H}_t^z) \to 0$. This ensures the observation latent captures the full causal effect of the action, while the action encoder learns a representation that is jointly denoised with the observation latent.
