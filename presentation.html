Motivation

The Latent Space Problem

Problem 1: Generic VAE Latents

DreamZero uses a frozen Wan2.1 VAE trained for generic video reconstruction

  • Retains textures, lighting, background clutter
  • Latent space confounds physical geometry with appearance
  • Wastes capacity on action-irrelevant details

Problem 2: Frozen Features

DINO-WM uses frozen DINOv2 features

  • Not trained to understand action effects
  • Agnostic to causal structure of manipulation
  • Discards action-relevant geometric info
Our approach: Fine-tune a pretrained video model's latent space to encode only the information that the history of interactions can predict — discarding unpredictable content while retaining action-relevant physics.

Background

World Models & DreamZero

DreamZero: autoregressive flow-matching transformer for joint video-action generation.

$$p_\theta(\mathbf{a}_t, \mathbf{o}_{t+1} \mid \mathbf{o}_{t-H+1:t}, \mathbf{a}_{t-H:t-1}, \mathbf{s}_t, \mathbf{l})$$

Architecture

  • Wan2.1 VAE encodes video to latent tokens
  • CausalWanModel (14B params) as DiT backbone
  • LoRA fine-tuned from pretrained image-to-video model
  • Blockwise causal attention with flow matching

Autoregressive Structure

  • At step \(t\): generate \((\mathbf{a}_t, \mathbf{o}_{t+1})\)
  • Action \(\mathbf{a}_t\) causes transition to \(\mathbf{o}_{t+1}\)
  • Closed-loop: predicted frames replaced with ground truth
  • Available at 14B and 1.3B scale
Key limitation: VAE is frozen — latent space was never optimized for action-relevant content.

Background

Unified Latents (UL)

UL (Heek et al. 2025): jointly train encoder + diffusion prior under the ELBO.

$$-\log p_\theta(\mathbf{x}) \leq \underbrace{-\mathbb{E}[\log p_\theta(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z}_0)]}_{\text{decoder}} + \underbrace{\text{KL}[p_\theta(\mathbf{z}_0|\mathbf{x}) \| p_\theta(\mathbf{z}_0)]}_{\text{prior}}$$

Encoder

Deterministic encoder + fixed noise:

$$\mathbf{z}_0 = \alpha_z(0)\,\mathbf{z}_{\text{clean}} + \sigma_z(0)\,\boldsymbol{\epsilon}_z$$

\(\alpha_z(0) \approx 1.0,\; \sigma_z(0) \approx 0.08\)

Prior Loss (KL bound)

$$\text{KL} \leq \mathbb{E}_\tau\!\left[w_{\text{KL}}(\tau)\,\|\mathbf{z}_{\text{clean}} - \hat{\mathbf{z}}_\theta\|^2\right] + C$$

Diffusion prior shapes the latent space by penalizing unpredictable content.

Key insight from UL: The prior loss creates an information bottleneck — encoder content that the prior cannot predict spikes the KL, incentivizing compact, predictable representations.

Background

Flow Matching

Alternative to DDPM: learn a velocity field along linear interpolation paths.

Linear Interpolation

$$\mathbf{x}_\tau = (1-\tau)\,\mathbf{x} + \tau\,\boldsymbol{\epsilon}$$

\(\tau=0\): clean data, \(\tau=1\): pure noise

Velocity Target

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{FM}} = \mathbb{E}\!\left[\|v_\theta(\mathbf{x}_\tau, \tau) - (\mathbf{x} - \boldsymbol{\epsilon})\|^2\right]$$

Equivalence to Reweighted Denoising

$$\|v_\theta - (\mathbf{x} - \boldsymbol{\epsilon})\|^2 = \frac{1}{\tau^2}\|\mathbf{x} - \hat{\mathbf{x}}_\theta\|^2$$

where \(\hat{\mathbf{x}}_\theta = \mathbf{x}_\tau + \tau\, v_\theta\)

Weighting Comparison

MethodWeight \(w(\tau)\)
KL-optimal (DDPM)\(\frac{1-\tau}{\tau^3}\)
Flow Matching\(\frac{1}{\tau^2}\)

Method

AC-UL: Two-Stage Pipeline

Stage 1: Fine-tune Encoder + Prior

  • Initialize from pretrained Wan2.1 VAE
  • Add learned action encoder \(A_\theta\) & action decoder \(D^a_\theta\)
  • Replace unconditional prior with history-conditioned joint dynamics prior
  • Fine-tune VAE + DiT with LoRA (rank 4)
  • Joint flow matching for obs + action latents
  • 500K training steps

Stage 2: Autoregressive DiT

  • Freeze all Stage 1 encoders/decoders
  • Train CausalWanModel (1.3B) on frozen AC-UL latents
  • LoRA fine-tuned from pretrained Wan2.1 weights
  • Joint video-action generation in latent space
  • Closed-loop inference with KV cache
  • Enables planning via latent rollouts

Stage 1: Physics-grounded vocabulary → Stage 2: Autoregressive generation & planning

Method — Stage 1

Latent Encoding & Joint Dynamics Prior

Encoders

$$\mathbf{z}_{k,\text{clean}} = E_\theta(\mathbf{o}_k) \in \mathbb{R}^{h \times w \times c}$$
$$\mathbf{b}_{k,\text{clean}} = A_\theta(\mathbf{a}_k) \in \mathbb{R}^{d_b}$$

Latent history:

$$\mathcal{H}_t^z = (\mathbf{l}, \mathbf{s}_t, \mathbf{z}_{t-H+1}, \mathbf{b}_{t-H}, \ldots, \mathbf{b}_{t-1}, \mathbf{z}_t)$$

Joint Dynamics Prior

Central contribution: replace unconditional prior with joint prediction:

$$(v_\theta^b,\; v_\theta^z) = \hat{f}_\theta^{\text{dyn}}(\mathbf{b}_{t,\tau},\; \mathbf{z}_{t+1,\tau},\; \mathcal{H}_t^z,\; \tau)$$
  • \(v_\theta^b\): velocity for current action (inverse dynamics)
  • \(v_\theta^z\): velocity for future observation (forward dynamics)

Forward Process (Flow Matching Interpolation)

$$\mathbf{z}_{t+1,\tau} = (1-\tau)\,\mathbf{z}_{t+1,\text{clean}} + \tau\,\boldsymbol{\epsilon}_z \qquad \mathbf{b}_{t,\tau} = (1-\tau)\,\mathbf{b}_{t,\text{clean}} + \tau\,\boldsymbol{\epsilon}_b$$

Same \(\tau\) shared between obs and action — enabling joint denoising.

Method — Stage 1

Information Bottleneck: Four-Way Pressure

Prior Loss \(\mathcal{L}_z^{\text{latent}}\)

Penalizes unpredictable content

If encoder preserves info the prior can't predict from history → obs MSE spikes

Pressure: DISCARD

Decoder Loss \(c_{\text{lf}} \cdot \mathcal{L}_x\)

Rewards information useful for reconstruction

Ensures latent retains fine geometric detail

Pressure: RETAIN

Action Loss \(\gamma \cdot \mathcal{L}_b\)

Prioritizes action-relevant structure

Inverse dynamics: latent transition must support action denoising

Pressure: ACTION-RELEVANT

Information Decomposition

$$I(\mathbf{z}_{t+1}; \mathbf{o}_{t+1}) = \underbrace{I(\mathbf{z}_{t+1}; \mathbf{o}_{t+1} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)}_{\text{unpredictable (penalized)}} + \underbrace{I(\mathbf{z}_{t+1}; \mathcal{H}_t^z)}_{\text{history-predictable (free)}}$$

Rate-Distortion Equilibrium

$$\mathbf{z}^* = \arg\min_{\mathbf{z}} \Big[\underbrace{I(\mathbf{z}; \mathbf{o}_{t+1} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)}_{\text{rate}} + c_{\text{lf}} \cdot \underbrace{D(\mathbf{o}_{t+1}, \hat{\mathbf{o}}_\theta)}_{\text{distortion}} - \gamma \cdot \underbrace{I(\mathbf{b}_t; \mathbf{z} \mid \mathcal{H}_t^z)}_{\text{action coupling}}\Big]$$

Method — Stage 1

Complete Stage 1 Objective

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{S1}} = \underbrace{\mathcal{L}_z^{\text{latent}} + \gamma \cdot \mathcal{L}_b}_{\text{joint dynamics prior (FM)}} + c_{\text{lf}} \cdot \underbrace{\mathcal{L}_x}_{\text{decoder}} + \underbrace{\mathcal{L}_a^{\text{recon}}}_{\text{action recon}}$$
Term Formula Role
\(\mathcal{L}_z^{\text{latent}}\) \(\mathbb{E}_\tau[\|v_\theta^z - (\mathbf{z}_{\text{clean}} - \boldsymbol{\epsilon}_z)\|^2]\) Obs latent velocity prediction (forward dynamics)
\(\gamma \cdot \mathcal{L}_b\) \(\gamma \cdot \mathbb{E}_\tau[\|v_\theta^b - (\mathbf{b}_{\text{clean}} - \boldsymbol{\epsilon}_b)\|^2]\) Action latent velocity prediction (inverse dynamics)
\(c_{\text{lf}} \cdot \mathcal{L}_x\) \(c_{\text{lf}} \cdot (\|\mathbf{o} - \hat{\mathbf{o}}\|^2 + \lambda_{\text{perc}} \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{perc}})\) Reconstruction via pretrained deterministic decoder
\(\mathcal{L}_a^{\text{recon}}\) \(\|\mathbf{a}_t - D^a_\theta(\mathbf{b}_{t,\text{clean}})\|^2\) Action encoder-decoder faithfulness
Interpretation: Prior loss discards unpredictable content. Decoder loss retains reconstruction-useful content. Action loss prioritizes action-relevant structure. Together: physics-grounded latent space.

Method — Stage 2

Autoregressive Generation & Planning

Closed-Loop Inference

  1. Encode: \(\mathbf{z}_{t} = E_\theta(\mathbf{o}_t)\)
  2. Generate: \((\hat{\mathbf{b}}_t, \hat{\mathbf{z}}_{t+1}) = \mathcal{T}_\phi(\mathcal{H}_t^z)\)
  3. Decode action: \(\hat{\mathbf{a}}_t = D^a_\theta(\hat{\mathbf{b}}_t)\)
  4. Execute: Apply \(\hat{\mathbf{a}}_t\) to robot
  5. Observe: Receive \(\mathbf{o}_{t+1}\)
  6. Replace: Update KV cache with true obs
  7. Advance \(t \leftarrow t+1\), repeat

Planning via Latent Rollouts

  1. Encode goal: \(\mathbf{z}_g = E_\theta(\mathbf{o}_g)\)
  2. Generate N=256 open-loop rollouts:
    $$(\hat{\mathbf{b}}_t, \hat{\mathbf{z}}_{t+1}) \to \cdots \to (\hat{\mathbf{b}}_{t+P-1}, \hat{\mathbf{z}}_{t+P})$$
  3. Score: \(d = \|\hat{\mathbf{z}}_{t+P} - \mathbf{z}_g\|^2\)
  4. Select best trajectory, execute first K actions
  5. Replan (receding horizon)
Planning in physics-grounded latent space — distance measures progress in physical state, not appearance.

Theory

Flow Matching Approximates KL Bound

Proposition 1: Velocity-to-Clean Equivalence

$$\|v_\theta(\mathbf{z}_\tau, \tau) - (\mathbf{z}_{\text{clean}} - \boldsymbol{\epsilon})\|^2 = \frac{1}{\tau^2}\,\|\mathbf{z}_{\text{clean}} - \hat{\mathbf{z}}_\theta\|^2$$

FM velocity MSE = reweighted clean-sample prediction MSE

Proposition 2: Same Minimizer, Different Weighting

\(\mathcal{L}^{\text{KL}} = \mathbb{E}_\tau[w_{\text{KL}}(\tau) \cdot \|\mathbf{z}_{\text{clean}} - \hat{\mathbf{z}}_\theta\|^2]\)

where \(w_{\text{KL}}(\tau) = \frac{1-\tau}{\tau^3}\)

\(\mathcal{L}^{\text{FM}} = \mathbb{E}_\tau[w_{\text{FM}}(\tau) \cdot \|\mathbf{z}_{\text{clean}} - \hat{\mathbf{z}}_\theta\|^2]\)

where \(w_{\text{FM}}(\tau) = \frac{1}{\tau^2}\)

Ratio: \(w_{\text{FM}}/w_{\text{KL}} = \tau/(1-\tau)\) — bounded, same minimizer \(\theta^*\)

Why the Bottleneck is Preserved

  • Same minimizer: Both reach minimum at the same \(\theta^*\)
  • Same gradient direction: Both compute \(\nabla_\theta L(\tau)\) scaled by positive constant
  • Monotonic penalty: Unpredictable content increases \(L(\tau)\) → increases loss under any positive weight

Theory

AC-UL as Approximate Variational Bound

$$-\log p_\theta(\mathbf{a}_t, \mathbf{o}_{t+1} \mid \mathcal{H}_t) \;\lessapprox\; \mathcal{L}_z^{\text{latent}} + \gamma \cdot \mathcal{L}_b + c_{\text{lf}} \cdot \mathcal{L}_x + \mathcal{L}_a^{\text{recon}}$$

Three Relaxations from Ideal UL

Component Ideal UL AC-UL Effect
Prior weighting \(w_{\text{KL}}(\tau) = \frac{1-\tau}{\tau^3}\) \(w_{\text{FM}}(\tau) = \frac{1}{\tau^2}\) Different gradient distribution; same minimizer
Decoder Diffusion decoder (tight bound) Deterministic MSE + perceptual Approximate reconstruction term
Encoder Stochastic (\(\sigma(0)>0\), finite KL) Deterministic (\(\mathbf{z}_0 = \mathbf{z}_{\text{clean}}\), KL = \(\infty\)) No formal ELBO; FM is practical proxy

Despite these gaps: The information bottleneck is preserved because all three changes maintain the property that encoding unpredictable content increases the loss. The bottleneck requires only that \(L(\tau) \geq 0\) and \(w(\tau) > 0\) — both hold for FM.

Analysis

What Do AC-UL Latents Encode?

Predictable Content (Retained)

  • Action-caused geometry: gripper closing on target after "pick up red block"
  • Object state changes: drawer opening after pull action
  • Relative gripper-to-target distance: explained by action trajectory + language goal

vs. DreamZero

  • Same Wan architecture & autoregressive paradigm
  • DreamZero (14B): frozen VAE retains everything
  • AC-UL (1.3B): fine-tuned VAE retains only physics
  • Better latents compensate for 10x fewer params

Unpredictable Content (Discarded)

  • Camera auto-exposure: brightness shifts have no causal link to actions
  • Specular reflections: high-freq appearance hard to predict
  • External disturbances: another person's hand not explained by action history

vs. FLARE

  • FLARE targets = frozen SigLIP-2 (general semantics)
  • AC-UL targets = learned action-predictable physics
  • FLARE: actions only, future features auxiliary
  • AC-UL: jointly generates observations + actions
Key insight: Latent space adaptation > model scale. Representation quality outweighs raw parameters.

Critical Commentary

Theoretical Concerns

1. No Formal ELBO — The "\(\lessapprox\)" Problem

The paper acknowledges three relaxations that prevent a formal variational bound. This is more serious than presented:

  • Deterministic encoder makes KL = \(\infty\): At \(\tau=0\), the encoder produces a Dirac delta. The KL between a Dirac and any Gaussian is infinite. This isn't just "approximate" — the ELBO literally does not exist in this formulation. The "\(\lessapprox\)" notation obscures this.
  • The "information bottleneck" argument is heuristic: The paper argues that FM loss increases with unpredictable content (true), but this is a directional argument, not a bound. Without a formal bound, there's no guarantee the bottleneck is tight enough to produce meaningfully compressed representations.
  • FM weighting emphasizes wrong regime: \(w_{\text{FM}}/w_{\text{KL}} = \tau/(1-\tau) \to \infty\) as \(\tau \to 1\). FM over-weights high-noise (pure denoising) and under-weights low-noise (fine structure). For action-relevant geometry, low-noise precision may matter more.

2. Proposition 3 is Not Really a Proposition

It states an approximate bound with "\(\lessapprox\)". In formal mathematics, a proposition should state something precise. This is more honestly described as a motivation or analogy with the ELBO, not a mathematical result. The proof reduces to "if we used DDPM + stochastic encoder, it would be exact" — but AC-UL uses neither.

Critical Commentary

Methodological Limitations

3. Two-Stage Training: Complexity vs. Elegance

  • Stage 1 requires jointly training VAE + DiT + action encoder/decoder. Stage 2 re-trains the DiT on frozen latents. This is 2x the training compute of single-stage DreamZero.
  • The frozen encoder at Stage 2 means any mistakes in Stage 1 are permanent. If the encoder converges to a suboptimal trade-off (e.g., too aggressive compression), Stage 2 cannot recover.
  • End-to-end training would be more principled but is claimed to be unstable. The paper doesn't explore this sufficiently.

4. Experimental Tables are Empty

  • All experimental tables contain placeholder values ("--"). No actual results are reported. The claim "1.3B AC-UL matches 14B DreamZero" is stated in the abstract and introduction but not supported by data.
  • This makes the paper currently a theoretical/methodological proposal, not an empirical contribution.

5. VAE Fine-tuning Risks

  • LoRA rank 4 on the VAE is extremely low. It's unclear if this provides enough expressiveness to meaningfully restructure the latent space, or if the changes are too subtle to matter.
  • No analysis of what changes in the VAE latent space after fine-tuning (e.g., latent space visualization, CKA similarity before/after).

Critical Commentary

Suggestions for Improvement

Theoretical Fixes

  • Add minimum noise \(\sigma(0)>0\): Even a small amount (as in original UL) would make the KL finite and the ELBO formally valid. This is a simple fix that costs almost nothing.
  • Drop "Proposition 3": Replace with honest framing: "AC-UL is inspired by the ELBO but is not a formal bound." The information bottleneck argument stands on its own without pretending to be a bound.
  • Analyze the FM/KL gap empirically: Measure how much the objectives actually differ during training. If \(w_{\text{FM}} \approx w_{\text{KL}}\) in the relevant range, show it.

Experimental Improvements

  • Run the experiments: The paper's value hinges entirely on whether 1.3B AC-UL actually matches 14B DreamZero. Without results, the contribution is speculative.
  • Latent space analysis: Visualize what changes. Show t-SNE/UMAP of latents before/after AC-UL, colored by action or task. Show Jacobian sensitivity maps.
  • Single-stage ablation: Try end-to-end training and report why it fails (if it does). The two-stage design needs justification beyond "it's easier."
  • Scaling analysis: Does AC-UL's advantage diminish at larger model scales? Test 5B AC-UL vs. 14B DreamZero.
  • More benchmarks: DROID and Bridge-v2 are both tabletop manipulation. Test on CALVIN, RLBench, or locomotion to show generality.

Summary

Takeaways & Future Directions

What AC-UL Proposes

  • Apply UL variational objective to fine-tune video model latent spaces for action-relevant content
  • History-conditioned joint dynamics prior creates information bottleneck
  • Four-way pressure (prior + decoder + action + action recon) yields physics-grounded representations
  • Two-stage pipeline: adapt latent space, then train autoregressive model on adapted latents

Core Hypothesis

Representation quality > model scale: 1.3B with better latents can match 14B with generic latents.

Future Directions

  • Scale Stage 2 to foundation-model sizes with AC-UL vocabulary
  • Incorporate action-free video for broader pretraining of the prior
  • Extend to multi-step chunk-wise generation for real-time control
  • Formal stochastic encoder variant with valid ELBO
  • Cross-domain transfer beyond tabletop manipulation
